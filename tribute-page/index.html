Muhammad Ali Jinnah

The man who saved Muslims of Subcontinent

Here's a time line of Muhammad Ali Jinnah's life:

  • 1876 - Born in Karachi, Sindh
  • 1895 - At age 19, he became the youngest British Indian to be called to the bar in England. Jinnah studied at Bombay University and at Lincoln's Inn in London.
  • 1913 - Jinnah left Indian National Congress and joined All India Muslim League
  • 1916 - He was elected as the president of All India Muslim League
  • 1920 - The Indian National Congress launched a movement of non-cooperation to boycott all aspects of British rule. Jinnah opposed this policy and resigned from the congress. There were by now profound differences between the congress and the Muslim League.
  • 1940 - At a Muslim League session in Lahore, the first official demand was made for the partition of India and the creation of a Muslim state of Pakistan.
  • 1947 - His insistence on this issue through negotiations with the British government resulted in the partition of India and the formation of the state of Pakistan on 14 August 1947.
  • 1947 - Jinnah became the first governor general of Pakistan.
  • 1948 - Jinnah died of tuberculosis on 11 September 1948.

"Few individuals significantly alter the course of history. Fewer still modify the map of the world. Hardly anyone can be credited with creating a nation-state. Muhammad Ali Jinnah did all three"

-- American Historian Stanely Wolpert
